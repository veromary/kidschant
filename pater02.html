Pater noster part 2

Middle bit

fiat
Be it done
voluntas
Will. As human beings, created in God's image and likeness, we have the ability to know and to love. Our intellect does the knowing and our will does the loving. Our will is what we use to choose good or evil. Here we ask that God's Will be done - we want to want what God wants.
tua
tuo, tua, tuum, all mean THY - the difference is that they describe masculine, feminine or neuter nouns.
sicut
As.
in caelo
In heaven. Last part we learned in caelis means in heaven. Technically caelis is the plural (heavens) and caelo is singular, but when we're translating the difference is a bit fuzzy.
et
and
in terra
On earth.
panem
Bread
nostrum
Our. Noster, nostra and nostrum all mean our - the difference is for describing masculine, feminine and neuter nouns.
quotidianum
Daily. Die means Day.
da
Give
nobis
To us.
hodie
This day. Hoc means this, and die means day.
